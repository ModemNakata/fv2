This page outlines FeVid.cloud's regulatory compliance frameworks, content reporting mechanisms, and law enforcement protocols.

1. 18 U.S.C. § 2257 Compliance Notice

All material elements of FeVid.cloud are compliant with the record-keeping requirements of Title 18 U.S.C. § 2257, § 2257A, and 28 C.F.R. 75.

FeVid.cloud operates strictly as an intermediary web host and secondary producer. We do not personally produce, film, or direct any of the visual depictions found on this service. All content is uploaded by independent, third-party creators.

As a condition of account creation and monetization, all Creators on FeVid.cloud represent and warrant that they maintain primary records containing full legal names, dates of birth, and official government identification for every single individual portrayed in their content, verifying that all participants are 18 years of age or older.

Inquiries regarding record locations or compliance data can be directed to our designated administrator at: nakatamodem@disroot.org

2. Notice and Action Policy (EU Digital Services Act)

In accordance with the EU Digital Services Act (DSA) and international content moderation standards, FeVid provides a transparent mechanism for users to report illegal material or content that violates our Acceptable Use Policy.

How to Report Content

If you encounter illegal material, non-consensual content, or other severe violations, please submit an electronic report to nakatamodem@disroot.org. Your report must include:

  • Exact Location: The specific FeVid.cloud URL(s) where the allegedly illegal content is located.
  • Explanation: A clear description of why the content is illegal or violates our policies, along with the legal basis if applicable.
  • Good Faith Declaration: A statement that you believe in good faith that the information and allegations contained in the notice are accurate.

Review Process

All submitted reports are evaluated by our human Trust & Safety team. If the content is found to be in violation, we will act expeditiously to remove or disable access to it. We will notify the reporting party of the outcome of our review.

Creator Redress & Appeals

If a Creator's content is removed following a notice, we will provide a "Statement of Reasons" explaining the removal. Creators who believe their content was removed in error or maliciously flagged have the right to submit an appeal via nakatamodem@disroot.org for a secondary human review.

3. Law Enforcement Guidelines

FeVid.cloud respects the rule of law while protecting the privacy of our users through data minimization and cryptocurrency infrastructure. This section outlines our protocols for handling data requests from government and law enforcement agencies.

Legal Threshold for Compliance

We only process and respond to data requests that are accompanied by a valid, legally binding subpoena, search warrant, or court order issued by a court of competent jurisdiction. Voluntary requests for data will be denied.

Data Limitations

Due to our privacy-centric architecture, law enforcement must understand our technical limitations before submitting requests:

  • Subscriber Data: We do not collect or store the real names, physical addresses, bank account details, or traditional credit card information of Subscribers.
  • Financial Transactions: All payments are processed on decentralized cryptocurrency networks (including privacy-preserving protocols). We only retain transaction hashes (TXIDs) to verify platform access. We cannot reverse, freeze, or intercept blockchain transactions.
  • Creator Data: We retain the specific identity verification records submitted by Creators required for legal compliance, which may be securely provided under a valid warrant.

How to Submit a Request

Law enforcement officials may submit formal legal requests to nakatamodem@disroot.org.

  • The request must originate from an official government or law enforcement email domain.
  • The request must clearly identify the target account using a specific email address, FeVid username, or exact platform URL.
  • Please allow up to 14 business days for our legal team to review and process valid requests.
